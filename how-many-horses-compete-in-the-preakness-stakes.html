Overview of the Preakness Stakes

The Preakness Stakes is an American horse race that forms the second leg of the esteemed Triple Crown of Thoroughbred Racing, which also includes the Kentucky Derby and the Belmont Stakes.

This flat race takes place annually in mid-May at the Pimlico Race Course in Baltimore, Maryland.

It's a 13/16-mile (about 1,900-meter) race for three-year-old Thoroughbred horses, with fillies carrying 121 pounds (55 kg) and colts carrying 126 pounds (57 kg).

The Preakness Stakes has a rich history, dating back to its establishment at Pimlico in 1873, and has witnessed remarkable moments and legendary champions over the years.
