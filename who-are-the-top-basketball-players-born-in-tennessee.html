Who are the Top Basketball Players Born in Tennessee?

Tennessee, a state known for its rich basketball tradition, has produced some of the most talented basketball players in history. From high school standouts to NBA superstars, the Volunteer State has made a significant impact on the world of basketball. In this article, we will explore the top basketball players born in Tennessee, highlighting their achievements and contributions to the sport.

Anfernee "Penny" Hardaway

Born in Memphis, Tennessee, Anfernee "Penny" Hardaway is considered one of the greatest basketball players to come out of the state. He had a successful college career at the University of Memphis before being drafted third overall by the Orlando Magic in the 1993 NBA Draft. Hardaway quickly became a fan favorite, showcasing his incredible athleticism and versatility on the court. He was a four-time NBA All-Star and played a crucial role in leading the Magic to the NBA Finals in 1995.

Bernard King

Bernard King, born in Brooklyn, New York, but raised in Tennessee, is widely regarded as one of the greatest pure scorers in NBA history. King attended the University of Tennessee, where he established himself as a dominant force on the court. After a successful college career, he was drafted seventh overall by the New York Nets in the 1977 NBA Draft. King went on to have a stellar NBA career, earning four All-Star selections and becoming a scoring champion in 1985.

Chris Paul

Chris Paul, born in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, but raised in Lewisville, North Carolina, is another standout basketball player who spent his high school years in Winston-Salem, Tennessee. Paul is widely regarded as one of the best point guards of his generation. He attended Wake Forest University before being selected fourth overall by the New Orleans Hornets (now the Pelicans) in the 2005 NBA Draft. Paul's exceptional court vision, leadership, and scoring ability have earned him numerous accolades, including numerous All-Star selections and All-NBA Team honors.

Allan Houston

Born in Louisville, Kentucky, but raised in Tennessee, Allan Houston had a remarkable basketball career. He played college basketball at the University of Tennessee, where he became one of the most prolific scorers in school history. Houston was selected 11th overall by the Detroit Pistons in the 1993 NBA Draft. He is best known for his time with the New York Knicks, where he became one of the team's all-time leading scorers. Houston was a two-time NBA All-Star and played a crucial role in leading the Knicks to the NBA Finals in 1999.

Dale Ellis

Dale Ellis, born in Marietta, Georgia, but raised in Tennessee, is another notable basketball player hailing from the Volunteer State. Ellis attended the University of Tennessee, where he had a successful college career. He was selected ninth overall by the Dallas Mavericks in the 1983 NBA Draft. Ellis played for several NBA teams throughout his career and was known for his exceptional shooting ability. He was a two-time NBA All-Star and won the NBA Most Improved Player Award in 1987.

Reggie Johnson

Reggie Johnson, born and raised in Memphis, Tennessee, had a standout basketball career in college. He played for the University of Tennessee, where he became one of the program's all-time leading scorers. Johnson was selected 15th overall by the San Antonio Spurs in the 1980 NBA Draft. Although his NBA career was cut short due to injuries, Johnson's impact on the court was undeniable during his college years.

Tennessee has produced a plethora of talented basketball players who have left a lasting impact on the sport. From legendary scorers like Bernard King and Allan Houston to versatile playmakers like Penny Hardaway and Chris Paul, these players have represented the state with pride and achieved great success at both the college and professional levels. Their contributions to the game will forever be remembered, solidifying Tennessee's place in basketball history.
