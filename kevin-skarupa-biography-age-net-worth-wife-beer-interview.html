Kevin Skarupa Biography

Kevin Skarupa is an American media personality and  a Meteorologist. He is known for his appearance on News 9 Daybreak with Erin Fehlau and Sean McDonald, then on News 9 at Noon. Kevin Skarupa, can be seen weekdays from 5 to 7 a.m. Skarupa, who is WMUR’s morning weather man, is also the producer of Cooks Corner, a show that airs Monday, Wednesday, and Fridays at noon and features food news and recipes from around New Hampshire.

Education And Career

He is a very educated man. Kevin Skarupa is an alumni Lyndon State College in Vermont. He graduated with a degree in meteorology. He currently holds both the AMS Television Seal of Approval and the Certified Broadcast Meteorologist seal. Kevin has worked in a number of stations before. Among these stations include; KIMT in Mason City, Iowa; WPBF in West Palm Beach, Fla.; and WKRN in Nashville.

Back in the year 1998, he worked at WMUR. He was working in the station during the weekends. He was a weather broadcaster where he did weekend weather under the name “Kevin Joseph.”

His favorite part of the job is visiting dozens of schools a year and talking with students about weather. The toughest part is the alarm clock, which goes off around 1 a.m. Kevin enjoys playing golf, running and watching sports.

Kevin Skarupa Interview

Kevin Skarupa Beer

I actually got to go to the WMUR studios this week to see Prodigal Brewing being taped (it was one of the three sessions I was actually able to attend) as well as stumble and stutter my way through my own taping of a description of beer week. So I was able to personally meet with Mr Skarupa and gab about beer and home brewing.

It turns out that there a number of employees who home brew. They have even considered starting their own club but with the crazy schedule they work it hasn’t come to fruition yet. At the end of my time there I left with some of Kevin’s beer (The Pop Corn Pilsner was very good) and a firm belief that home brewers will rule the world one day.

Interview

Q: How did you get started home brewing?

Skarupa: My wife bought me a brew kit 5 years ago for my birthday, it was love at first brew from there!

Q: How often do you get to brew?

Skarupa: I brew when I can, I must have made 4 or 5 brews last year

Q: Do you have a favorite style of beer?

Skarupa: I am lucky that my palette likes and can appreciate most beer styles. What’s great is I probably have only scratched the surface on all the different kinds and styles across the world…a hobby you can have for life!

Q: Do you belong to any NH Home brew Clubs?

Skarupa: I don’t belong to any…yet. I have a feeling over the next few years as my interest grows further,
that will be the logical next step. I listen to a lot of beer podcasts and the options are endless.

Q: What is it that you like most about home brewing?

Skarupa: I really, really enjoy the brew day itself. I started out like most, doing kits to learn the process. Now
I like to tinker with established recipes and make them my own. It is truly a lot of fun!

Q: What was the last beer you brewed?

Skarupa: A Popcorn Pilsner was the last one I brewed (this a clone from Sun King Brewing mentioned on the
Brewing Network episode date 02-13-2012)

Q: What are your favorite New Hampshire beers?

Skarupa: My top 3 in no particular order…Throwback’s Spicy Bohemian, Milly’s Pumpkin, and Woodstock
Inn’s Maple Porter.
