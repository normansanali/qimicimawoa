Tiffini Hale Net Worth

Tiffini Hale had quite a successful run on the show which helped to add on her burgeoning net worth of at least $400 thousand. She appeared in “The All-New Mickey Mouse Club” for its entire run from 1989-1995, though her appearances became sporadic after the show’s second season, per IMDb.

In 1990, Tiffini was chosen by channel executives — along with Magno, Hampton, Jeune, Pampolina, and Pierre — to become part of The Party. It was the first group signed to Disney’s (then new) Hollywood Records label.

The band issued two studio albums and three compilation albums that included outtakes, remixes, and best hits, with a rendition of Dokken’s “In My Dreams” becoming their biggest chart smash. On the Billboard Hot 100, the song reached No. 34.

In 1994, Tiffini reunited with Hale to co-host the final season of The All-New Mickey Mouse Club, which featured cast members such as Britney Spears, Justin Timberlake, Ryan Gosling, and Christina Aguilera.

The Party – an acronym for Positive Attitude Reflects Today’s Youth – was responsible for early 1990s songs like “I Found Love” and “That’s Why.”  The group appeared on a 1991 episode of “The Party Machine” with Nia Peeples and a 1993 episode of “Blossom,” while the song “Summer Vacation” was featured in a 1991 episode of “Doogie Howser, M.D.”  The group went on hiatus from 1997-2013, though Hale did not rejoin when the band returned to performing.

However, Tiffini did not take part in a reunion of The Party in 2013, nor in a 2019 30th anniversary reunion for MMC.
